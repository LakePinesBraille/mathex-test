Lesson 5: Math within Text

This lesson shows how to create math formulas embedded within literary text.

Example 1: Create an inline formula.

You can include a math formula within a paragraph by typing Ctrl‑m at the point in the text where you want the math to appear.

In the equation y m x b the letter m represents the slope of the line.

When you finish the formula, typing Ctrl+Shift+m returns you to text mode.

The space key will also return you to text mode, as spaces are not used when entering math formulas.

This type of formula is known as an inline formula because it appears on the baseline within surrounding text.

Example 2: Create a display formula.

You can create a math formula separated from surrounding paragraphs by typing Ctrl‑Alt‑m to enter display math mode.

the letter m represents the slope of the line.

When you finish the formula, typing Enter returns you to text mode and starts a new paragraph.

This type of equation is known as a display formula since it is visually separated from surrounding paragraphs.
